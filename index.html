I'm delighted to have you visit my personal webpage. Allow me to introduce myself. I am currently a Ph.D. student in the Department of Agriculture and Applied Economics at the University of Georgia. My research interests lie in the captivating realms of economic growth pertaining to structural transformation. I possess a keen enthusiasm for employing big data to address economic inquiries, interlinking microscopic pixels to provide a more dynamic perspective and elucidate macro phenomena.

Before embarking on my doctoral journey, I served as a research analyst at the esteemed International Monetary Fund (IMF) in the Africa Department, focusing on southern African countries. Alongside my daily responsibilities, I actively engaged in diverse projects, ranging from early detection of economic crises to identifying game-changing climate policies. These experiences fostered a deep appreciation for the intricate interplay between economics and global affairs.

My educational background encompasses a Master of Science degree in Data Analytics and Policies from Johns Hopkins University, where I enhanced my proficiency in harnessing the power of data to inform policy decisions. Complementing this, my academic foundation was laid at Hong Kong Baptist University, where I obtained a Bachelor of Business Administration (BBA) degree in Applied Economics.
